Fulfilling Yaakov’s Dream – Art, Memory, and a Future in the Galilee

In the village of Kfar Shamai, a new Art and Therapy centre is being established in memory of Yaakov Ozeri. A space of insperation trough creativity, connections and growth.

Join us in bringing the center to life.

A Home for Creativity, Therapy, and Community

The center will promote culture and art in the northern periphery, serving as an open and accessible space for all ages to create, learn, connect, and grow through art.

The center will host art workshops, therapy groups, exhibitions, lectures, and performances. The unique structure and the open space around it will provide an experiential and professional environment that enables creation, connection, and dialogue.

Seeing Far, Seeing Clearly

A design inspired by Yaakov’s vision device — a symbol of foresight and dreams.

The building will feature round spaces, transparent walls, outdoor creative areas, an intimate amphitheater, a graden for artists, and modular rooms — all designed with a spirit of openness, accessibility, and community.
A center that places the periphery at the heart of cultural life.

View the 360° Renderings of the Center

The center’s location in Kfar Shamai is no coincidence—it is the landscape of Yaakov’s childhood, where he grew, dreamed, and created.

Choosing this site is not only a geographic decision but a continuation of Yaakov’s vision: to transform home and roots into a source of creativity and inspiration for all.

A Vision Coming to Life

With the support of partners and a growing community.

The "Dream Like Yaakov" Center is currently under development. Born from a deeply personal and moving vision, the project is backed by professional planning and wide support from institutional, community, and philanthropic partners in Israel and around the world.

We are in the final stage — your support will help transform the dream into a living space for art, therapy, and inspiration.

With heartfelt thanks to our supporters

Yaakov Ozeri

From a young age, Yaakov Ozeri, of blessed memory, was a creator, a designer, and a soul deeply connected to art. As an adult, he studied Industrial Design at Shenkar College and saw art as a universal language—open and connecting for everyone, at every age and place. Yaakov believed deeply in the power of creativity to connect, to heal, and to bring change—and lived by this belief in every step he took.
